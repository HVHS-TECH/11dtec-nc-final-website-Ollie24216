How to play badminton

Badminton is played by hitting a shuttlecock over a net using a racket, trying to land it in your opponent’s court so they can’t return it. You start each rally with a serve, hitting the shuttle diagonally into the other side. During the game, you move quickly around the court, using different shots like clears (high and far), drops (soft and close to the net), and smashes (fast and powerful) to outplay your opponent. Good footwork helps you reach the shuttle in time, and quick reactions help you return it accurately. The goal is to win points by making the shuttle land inside the opponent’s court.

🏸 Serving Ruless

  • The serve must be hit underarm (below your waist)
  • The racket head must be pointing downwards when you hit the shuttle
  • Both feet must stay on the ground during the serve
  • You must serve diagonally into the opponent’s service box
  • The shuttle must land inside the correct service area
  • Only one serve attempt is allowed
  • In doubles, players take turns serving after winning a point.
