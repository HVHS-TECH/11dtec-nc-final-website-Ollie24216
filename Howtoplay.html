🏸 How to Play Badminton

Learn the basics of the game

What is Badminton?

Badminton is a racket sport played with a shuttlecock and two or four players. The aim is to hit the shuttle over the net and land it in your opponent’s court without them returning it.

It is a fast-paced sport that requires speed, accuracy, and good reflexes. Players must constantly move around the court and react quickly to different shots.

Basic Rules

A match starts with a serve. The serve must be hit underarm and below the waist. The shuttle must travel diagonally into the opponent’s service box.

Players score a point when the opponent fails to return the shuttle or hits it out of bounds. A game is usually played to 21 points, and you must win by 2 clear points.

Skills Needed

To become good at badminton, players need strong footwork, quick reactions, and good hand-eye coordination. Smashes are used for powerful attacking shots, while drops and clears are used for control and strategy.

Tips for Beginners

- Always stay lighter on your feet so you can move quickly
- Watch the shuttle carefully at all times
- Practice serving consistently
- Try to return to the centre of the court after every shot
